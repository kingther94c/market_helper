Regime-Aware Policy-Expert Allocation — Research Report

Economically-interpretable policy experts from Growth×Inflation regimes, an ex-ante ML predictor of which expert outperforms, and a soft mixture-of-experts allocation. Walk-forward, vs investable baselines.

Verdict: MONITOR
The ML mixture-of-experts improves risk-adjusted return over standard investable baselines (Sharpe 0.65 vs 0.58 for the best single static expert; max-drawdown -28.13% vs -42.92%), with the edge concentrated in crisis de-risking (2008, 2022). But a simple cash-in-stagflation heuristic achieves a comparable/better Sharpe (0.79), so the ML's marginal value over a well-chosen rule is modest. Deploy as an advisory signal, track live, reassess vs the simple rule. Read-only with respect to the broker.

1. Data & accounting

Monthly, 1989-02 .. 2026-05 (448 months). Sleeves: EQ = S&P 500 TR (^SP500TR), CM = S&P GSCI (^SPGSCI), FI = synthetic constant-maturity 10Y from FRED GS10 (par-bond duration/convexity), CASH = TB3MS. FI is a futures excess-return overlay. Accounting: R = cash·100% + Σ exposure·(sleeve−cash). A MACRO (TSMOM trend) sleeve was analysed (positive crisis-alpha every regime, see §2) but removed from the final experts: it was a uniform +10 overlay across all four, so it does not differentiate them and cancels in the cross-sectional allocation. Dropping it costs a small crisis-diversifying return (MoE Sharpe 0.69→0.65) but makes the experts directly implementable in EQ/CM/FI.

3. Labels & 4. Ex-ante predictor

Forward expert returns at 3 / 6 (primary) / 12 months → four label families (winner / winner-with-margin / softmax / direct cross-sectional excess). Predictor: 22 ex-ante features (point-in-time; macro lagged +1 month) across inflation / growth / policy / rates / credit / risk / momentum / stress / trend. Multi-output Ridge predicting forward expert returns; alpha by an embargoed time-series CV (heavy shrinkage is essential — a naive low-alpha fit overfits the autocorrelated overlapping targets and goes negative).

6. Robustness & limitations

  • Experts are full-sample oracle templates (the teacher step) — static vectors, but their regime dating is in-sample; only the predictor is walk-forward. Raw MoE return therefore inherits some in-sample optimism.
  • Heavy shrinkage required: the predictor only has positive skill with strong regularization (embargoed-CV alpha ≈1000-3000); low alpha overfits.
  • Stagflation is data-thin (2022 + 1990; the 1970s predate tradable data) — the most fragile conclusion; carries the heaviest caveat.
  • A simple cash-in-stagflation rule is competitive — much of the ML's value is automating crisis de-risking a heuristic also captures.
  • Frictionless: futures financing spread / transaction costs not yet charged (turnover is modest, ~6%/mo for the MoE).

7. What is robust vs tentative

  • robust directional inflation-up shorts duration / favors commodities + trend; growth drives EQ. MACRO trend is crisis alpha.
  • tentative template the exact expert exposure sizes (in-sample ceilings shrunk to round numbers).
  • statistically-supported signal the predictor's cross-sectional IC (≈0.2) with heavy shrinkage.
  • not yet implementation-ready deploy advisory-only; add cost/financing audit and live tracking before any capital decision.
