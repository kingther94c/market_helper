Q9 Macro Calibration — Neighborhood-Stability Addendum (Phase 1 + 2)

2026-05-23 · checks whether the Q9 winner sits on a parameter spike or in a stable basin. User-driven critique of grid-argmax selection.

Motivation: the Q9 winner was selected as the argmax of train_overall + 0.3·stability among configs that did not regress on holdout. Argmax of a noisy surface can sit on a single grid-point spike. A robust optimum is one whose neighborhood is also top-tier and has no particularly bad neighbor. This addendum checks Q9 against that criterion in two phases:
  1. Phase 1: re-analyze the existing 360-config grid with L1 neighborhood statistics (zero new computation).
  2. Phase 2: half-step refinement around the contenders (162 new configs, augmented grid = 522 configs), repeat the neighborhood analysis.

Selection rules (user-confirmed before execution)

  • Neighborhood: L1 grid-index, same layer_blend (categorical, not crossed), ±1 step on exactly one of the 4 continuous dims (ivw, gvw, gt, it). Max 8 neighbors per config.
  • Top metric: median(neighbor train_overall) — robust to a single outlier neighbor.
  • No bad neighbor: min(neighbor train) ≥ max(baseline_train, self − 5pp) — worst neighbor must beat baseline AND stay within 5pp of self.
  • Eligibility filters: n_neighbors ≥ 4 AND holdout ≥ baseline_holdout AND self_train > baseline_train AND no_bad_neighbor.
  • Ranking: robust_train = mean(self_train, neighbor_median_train); tiebreaker: self_train.
  • Train/holdout discipline preserved: all stats split by train and holdout; holdout never enters the optimization objective.

Recommended params (apply)

Caveats

  • Grid is still discrete: even at half-step, the response surface is sampled, not analytical. A truly continuous optimum could sit between Phase-2 grid points.
  • Small holdout (4 anchors): holdout delta of ±1-2pp is within sampling noise. Don't read holdout regressions of less than ~2pp as strong evidence.
  • Neighborhood definition is L1, not L2: configs that differ on 2+ dims are not considered neighbors. This is deliberate (each dim has different scale and meaning) but it means stability w.r.t. simultaneous multi-dim drift is not tested.
  • Layer blend is treated as categorical: we never compare across blends in a neighborhood. A robust optimum within m/m=0.6/0.4 may still be brittle if the operator changes blend for other reasons.
  • "5pp drop" floor is arbitrary: a tighter or looser threshold would change which configs pass the "no bad neighbor" rule. 5pp was chosen because it's roughly the Q8→Q9 train improvement scale.
